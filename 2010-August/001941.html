The config file I am testing with is the default generated by the suse-pxe-client config.xml . The virtualbox I am testing with does have a hard drive just to see if I can get any PXE boot build to startup successfully.

Following Denis' suggestion I changed my KIWI/config.default to:

IMAGE=/dev/ram1;suse-11.2-pxe-client.x86_64;1.2.8;192.168.16.8;4096
UNIONFS_CONFIG=/dev/ram2,/dev/ram1,clicfs

After rebuilding the image due to a md5 mismatch I am able to get the image booted into RAM. I then tried a modified config.xml and built a new image which boots great as well.

Thanks Denis, now I am going to try and convert my suse studio virtual appliance to 
pxe boot.


Thanks.
