> It may be just me, but if I were to look at the kiwi command line options 
> and see --list-xmlinfo I would not try this if I were looking for a list of 
> usable patterns. I think --list-patterns would be a more obvious way to get

It's surely not just you and I agree, the --list-xmlinfo is more
or less a dustbin for information at the moment. I would love to
restructure that but I need your help. The questions are:

- what information do you need from an image description
  most often ? and don't want to always open the config.xml
  to find out

- how should the result be presented ? Do people prefer a bunch
  of commandline switches like --info pattern , --info type, etc...
  or is it better to present all information at once in a
  specific format ?

For the moment the --list-xmlinfo provides the following
information:

a) the primary type
b) the source repository URL's
c) the install size for the prepare step (summary of solved package sizes)
d) the deletion size for packages marked as to be deleted
e) the available patterns according to the sources

Is there more information which is important to find
out before actually preparing/creating the image ?

Thanks for you input
