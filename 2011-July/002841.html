Hi all,

For one of the pilot-projects around here, they build a bootable encrypted USB-stick, just containing Gnome-desktop, LUKS, OpenSC, NX-client, ICA-client, openvpn, Linphone and some small stuff. Allthough i pointed them to kiwi, they decided in their infinite wisdom to do everything with Ubuntu and create some spagetty-code to create the images.....

Currently they find out that a lot of drievers for the latest USB-wifi-dongels are not present in the default ubuntu repo.
As long as the pilot runs, i've got the possibility to present another option:
- either let them continue, with U-11.04 (with the problems with Unity etc etc)
- OpenSuSE_11.4 default
- OpenSuSE_11.4 with kernel-of-the-day
- OpenSuSE_Tumbleweed.

So, if maximum number of drivers (VGA, wifi) is the goal,  which option gets me the best results?
At least Greg-KH works fulltime at kernels at SuSE. From Ubuntu i've heard that they obain most of their kernelpatches from other distro's, so i really wonder how fast they can produce new drivers...
I'm looking for an un-biased suggestion. (Obviously any SuSE-versions integrates best with kiwi, but I am allready pro-SuSE )
