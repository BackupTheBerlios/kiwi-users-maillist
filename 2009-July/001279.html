well your tarball puts everything below kiwiBrandingTest/ this
results in a root filesystem which has among other directories

  /kiwiBrandingTest/..

which is of course not critical but absolutely useless for
bootsplash and gfxboot. It's important that your branding
tarball _overwrites_ the data as they are expected by
bootsplash and gfxboot. So you have to provide a tarball
which contains data in:

  usr/share/gfxboot/  => for gfxboot
  /etc/bootsplash/    => for bootsplash

You should have a look into the packages

  bootsplash-branding-openSUSE
  gfxboot-devel  

there you see where they install data and with your custom
tarball you can overwrite that data to include your own
branding. But that of course requires that you use the
same directory structure
