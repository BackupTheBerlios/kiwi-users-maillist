Hi,

> Just for information - I have solved the problem with partition layout,
> I have now bootable usb stick with split filesystem and FAT partition,
> which is accesible from Windows machines.
> 
> The solution is quite simple, instead of rewriting Kiwi, I just have let
> Kiwi do the default partition layout (first partition squashfs, second
> ext3) and then I shrinked second partition and move both of them to the
> end of the stick. (I used cfdisk, resize2fs and dd to achieve it). I
> created FAT partition on the first part of stick.
> 
> Then I reinstalled grub with a new menu.lst config and unpacked initrd
> created by Kiwi, had a nice time reading it and changed three variables
> reffering to root partition of the stick, read only partition and
> read-write partition. Repacked initrd, copied it over and it worked.
> 
> Good night, maybe I will write something more detailed later.
