Hi,

> Our typical disk layout: hidden diagnostics, boot, boot, ext with swap
> and data. We use a single hard disk instead of multiple disks or
> other flash media.  We use 2 bootable partitions which we use
> for "live" updates where we flash into the unused partition then boot
> into it.  The separate boot partition is also used for flash failure
> fallback.  We can't clobber any of the partitions other than the unused
> boot partition.

Ah now I understand, quite cool concept :) It's clear to me now
why you only need a filesystem image and not more.

> We use our own 1'st stage bootloader which tweaks the partition table
> such that the OS always sees itself as partition 1 (/dev/sda1).

That sounds interesting how do you do that ? In former times I used
fdisk which allows to have the partition id's not in the same order
than the address range of the single partitions. Given this I could
have /dev/sda1 pointing to somewhere in an extended area. But I found
out that some bootloaders and nearly all other disk partition tools
don't like that. As I have to support parted with kiwi too I had to
change the code. 

I'm eager to hear how you do that :) ?
