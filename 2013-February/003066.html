Hi,

> > But bootstrapping on something non empty sounds
> > weird to me and by the way creates a system with
> > broken integrity
> In my case the root is almost empty: It contains some
> directories like /usr and /boot that are on separate
> filesystems.  I have to have these directories,
> but I don't think they lead to broken integrity.

If you start a system with just /usr and /boot it's broken
in the first place. Anyway you are saying you need data
from /usr and /boot. what data ? why not provide them as
an archive

   <archive name="some-name"/>

> I don't know how to solve this problem without
> either telling kiwi to accept the nonempty root
> or doing the bootstrapping completely manual.
> The latter one is what I want to avoid by using kiwi.
> Do you see any other way to reach my goal with kiwi?

If you really need to hook in /usr and /boot from somewhere
else you can do it the following way:

1) prepare a mini root system consisting only out of 
   bootstrap (no packages in the type="image" section

2) store that mini root system as the start, e.g in a tarball
   or somewhere else

3) mount /usr and /boot into that mini root system

4) make sure you have a working zypper now in that system
   chroot into it and test e.g zypper --help

5) use kiwi with --recycle-root of that mini root system
   and build your image

I think I don't have to mention that the risk that this system
is broken is pretty high because you are hooking in /usr from
somewhere. I wouldn't do that

just my2cents
