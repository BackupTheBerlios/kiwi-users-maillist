Hello,

I have created a working openSUSE 11.1 + KDE 4.2 (factory) on a 8 GB USB thumb 
drive. I use Yast's Image Creator to build the images. I think used kiwi to 
put the image(s) onto the thumb drive.

My plan is to use this thumb drive in machines that have intel, ati and nvidia 
video cards. The intel video cards are not a problem since Sax2[1] can detect 
it and pick the right driver. Also, as of lately the radeonhd driver avoids 
any problems with the ATI video cards since it supports all the ATI video 
cards that I use (no need for fglrx - YAY!). My problem is with the nVidia 
video cards. Currently, I install the nvidia driver from nvidia's repo. Is 
there a way to have Sax2 pick the nvidia driver instead of the nv driver?

My goal is to have a thumb drive that I plug into any of my machines and, with 
no console kung-fu, get into KDE with fully tricked out desktop effects. I can 
get pretty close with intel and ati video cards by running sax2[1] before 
calling startx. However, I would also like to do this with nvidia video cards.


Any tips would be great.
