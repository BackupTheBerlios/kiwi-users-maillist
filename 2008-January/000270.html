Here is my simple dhcpd.conf I use on my laptop for presenting
PXE boot:

option domain-name "example.org";
option domain-name-servers 192.168.200.2;
option broadcast-address 192.168.200.255;
option routers 192.168.200.2;
option subnet-mask 255.255.255.0;
default-lease-time 600;
max-lease-time 7200;
ddns-update-style none; ddns-updates off;
log-facility local7;

subnet 192.168.200.0 netmask 255.255.255.0 {
    filename "pxelinux.0";
    next-server 192.168.200.2;
    range dynamic-bootp 192.168.200.5 192.168.200.20;
}

and in /etc/sysconfig/dhcpd

DHCPD_INTERFACE="eth0"

of course the interface name, IP ranges and addresses needs to
be adapted according to your network
